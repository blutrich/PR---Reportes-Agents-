# Rise Up - Brand Rules

## Company Overview
- **Name:** RiseUp (רייזאפ)
- **Industry:** Fintech - Personal Financial Management
- **Founded:** 2017
- **HQ:** Tel Aviv, Israel
- **Key product/service:** App that helps families manage money, track spending, reduce overdraft
- **Founders:** Yuval Samet (CEO), Iftach Bar, Tamara Harel-Cohen, Hanan Rubin
- **Funding:** ~$50M raised at $130M valuation
- **Users:** Tens of thousands of Israeli families

## Key Messages
1. Israeli families can save over 1,000 shekels per month with better financial visibility — the banks have this data but don't share it
2. 25% of overdraft families turned positive after using RiseUp — proving the problem is information, not income
3. Financial stress destroys relationships — 80% of couples report reduced financial tension after using the service

## Spokesperson(s)
- **Yuval Samet, Co-founder & CEO** - available for quotes on: cost of living, overdraft crisis, open banking, financial literacy, family financial health
- Yuval speaks directly, uses numbers, avoids corporate jargon. He says "שלום בית" not "relationship wellness"

## Competitors (NEVER mention by name in briefs)
- Caspion
- FairFly
- Moneytree
- Any bank's own financial management tools

## Sensitive Topics (handle carefully)
- **April 2024 layoffs (50% of staff):** Mention only in background/context if relevant. Do not lead with it. Frame factually: "in April 2024, the company reduced staff by 50% due to war's impact on customer acquisition"
- **Valuation vs. revenue:** Don't speculate on revenue. Use funding/valuation numbers factually
- **"Replacing banks":** RiseUp complements banking, doesn't replace it. The angle is "banks should do this but don't" — not "banks are evil"

## Mandatory Disclaimers
- Company data should be attributed as "נתוני החברה" or "נתוני RiseUp"
- External data must have independent sources (CBS, Bank of Israel, etc.)
