# Reporter Profile: Gad Lior

## Identity
- **Full Name (Hebrew):** גד ליאור (שם משפחה מקורי: ליכטנשטיין)
- **Full Name (English):** Gad Lior
- **Born:** April 1953
- **Outlet:** Yedioth Ahronoth, ynet
- **Beat:** Economy, Budget, Policy, Finance Ministry, Bank of Israel
- **Years Active:** 50+ (since age 16 at Maariv, joined Yedioth 1979)
- **Notable:** First Israeli journalist in Saudi Arabia and UAE (1993). Covered 19 finance ministers and 7 Bank of Israel governors. Founded "Economic Communication" course at Open University. Head of Jerusalem desk since 1995.

## Signature Format
- **Column name/format:** "X הערות על Y" (X remarks on Y)
- **Typical length:** 500-800 words
- **Publication frequency:** Near-daily columns on ynet

## Structural Pattern

### 3 הערות (Argumentative)
| Part | Role | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| הערה 1 | "מה אומרים לך" - Official narrative, promise, or common assumption | "נתניהו וסמוטריץ' הבטיחו שהמסים יופחתו" |
| הערה 2 | "מה באמת קורה" - Data, numbers, internal sources that contradict | "בכירים באוצר: אפשר לשכוח מההבטחה" |
| הערה 3 | "מה זה אומר עליך" - Impact on citizen, moral conclusion | "החלשים תמיד ייפגעו והעשירים תמיד ירוויחו" |

### 5 הערות (Mosaic)
| Part | Role | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| Each הערה | Independent observation, different scale | One about macro policy, one about a stadium's parking, one about demographics |
| Mix | Personal memory + data + street-level | "לפני 47 שנים..." alongside population statistics |

## Thinking Patterns

1. **Promise vs Reality:** Opens with what politicians said, then demolishes it with data. Lets the gap speak for itself without over-editorializing.

2. **Numbers as Weapons:** Never uses adjectives when a number will do. "מיליארד שקל ליום", "0.000040 שקל", "$526 לנפש". The number IS the argument.

3. **Weak vs Strong (The Lobbyist Test):** For any policy, asks: who has lobbyists, who doesn't? "לציבור העובדים פשוט אין לוביסטים."

4. **Cause-and-Effect Chains:** Thinks in dominoes: war -> deficit -> taxes -> purchasing power drops -> middle class shrinks.

5. **International Mirror:** Uses other countries' data to reflect Israel back at itself. Yemen GDP vs Israel GDP. Norwegian fund divesting. EU threatening sanctions.

6. **Internal Sources:** "בכירים באוצר", "גורמים בבנק ישראל" - unnamed but clearly real. His 40-year network IS the story.

## Voice Rules

1. "אנחנו/עלינו" not "הם" - stands WITH the reader, looking AT the system. "שיוטלו עלינו", "הפראיירים שישלמו".

2. Numbers, not adjectives. "42 מיליארד שקל", not "סכום עצום".

3. Direct quotes in quotation marks, even short ones. "אפשר לשכוח מההבטחה הזאת."

4. Short sentences. No academic jargon. No flowery language.

5. No emojis. Almost no exclamation marks. The data shouts.

6. Skeptical of official statistics. "יש ספקנות משמעותית לגבי שיעור האינפלציה המדווח."

7. Active voice always. Not "הוחלט ש..." but "הממשלה החליטה ש..."

## What Gad Lior Would Never Do

1. Write a balanced "on the other hand" piece without a clear position
2. Use English words when Hebrew ones exist
3. Write without current data or fabricate numbers
4. Hide behind passive voice
5. End a column passively - always pushes toward a conclusion
6. Praise the government without irony
